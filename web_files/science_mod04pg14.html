Find Chemical Information:
Spectra Library Databases

In general, library databases such as SciFinder and Reaxys will cover more compounds than free web resources. These databases are also good for structure and reaction searching:

SciFinder
+700,000 experimental (MS, IR, 13C-NMR, H-NMR) spectra and 23.8 million predicted H-NMR spectra. Please Note: You will need to register for access to SciFinder with your UCLA email address.

Watch the tutorial on the right to learn how to find spectra in SciFinder.

Reaxys
Literature references for spectra for many of the 11 million organic compounds. Note: Reaxys contains references and links to journal articles where you will find spectra. You must click through to the full text of the article to find it.

For more, see the Spectra Resources Page on the Chemistry Research Guide.
