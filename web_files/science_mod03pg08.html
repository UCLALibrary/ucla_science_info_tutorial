What is Grey literature?

  • contains preliminary data and new/untested ideas
  • is not peer-reviewed, refereed or held to rigorous standards
  • is intended for a small audience
  • is often published by non-traditional publishers (museum publications, internal publications, etc.)

Grey literature formats include (but are not limited to):

  • Conference proceedings
  • Poster sessions
  • Abstracts of presented talks
  • Dissertations/theses
  • Pre-prints

For key resources in grey literature, see the Science and Engineering Library website which lists resources for dissertations, standards, patents, technical reports, safety/MSDS sources, and vendor catalogs.
